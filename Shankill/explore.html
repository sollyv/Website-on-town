Heritage Sites

LeadMines Way & Ballycorus Chimney


A lovely, easy walk of an afternoon on good paths through the woodland will bring you to the lead mines Ballycorus chimney.There are some amazing views from the summit and believe it or not, you are still in Dublin. It seems a far cry from the city below. The chimney is one for the archaeological lovers, it was built in the 19th century to dispel the fumes from the lead works at the bottom of the hill.

Pucks Castle


Puck’s Castle is an old 3-storey tower whose walls have stood the test of time… Charged with a rather serious charm, the building is lost in the middle of the countryside, and fascinates as much as it can disturb… Puck’s Castle is named after the ghost that has haunted the castle since its beginnings… Stories proving his existence are running around the country, to the delight of lovers of tales and legends… Frightening, isn’t it? For the more pragmatic, the castle is the bearer of a high historical episode: this castle would have formerly served as a refuge for King James II (also called James II) and his army, during their flight from the Battle of the Boyne. A fact far from being insignificant, which gives an additional cachet to the castle! It was built from sacred stones culled from the nearby Bearna Dhearg, however there is very little of the castle left intact. But inside you can see evidence of the stone stairwell and the fireplace. It’s worth a trip if interested in the history.

Shanganagh Castle


The Shanganagh Castle is a fortress built in 1408 by the Lawless family… A bit austere, it imposes itself by its imposing architecture.

Located near Mill Lane in Shankill, Shanganagh Castle would have been inhabited by his descendants until 1763 .
It would then have been the victim of a terrible fire in 1783, transforming the fortress into a veritable heap of ruins… During the late 18th century, a manor house of the same name was rebuilt on vast lands on Shankill’s border with County Wicklow. It is believed to have been used as a juvenile prison between 1969 and 2002.

Nature Walks

Rathmichael Wood



Rathmichael Walk is a short looped walk through the woodland. On reaching the hill fort site the walker is rewarded with splendid views, northwards over Dublin bay, eastwards towards Killiney and Dalkey and southwards to Bray head and the Little Sugar Loaf. This is a looped walk of 1.7km and will take approximately 30minutes.

Shankill to the Scalp Loop



A lovely hike along the eastern section of the Dublin Mountain’s Way from Shankill to the Scalp, returning to Shankill via Carrickgollogan summit and the Lead Mine’s chimney.

For more information visit
info@dublinmountains.ie

Carrickgollogan Walk



There are two walking trails in the wood and the Dublin Mountains Way also has its route through Carrickgollogan.

-The Lead Mines Way (2 kms, 40mins, easy) is a gentle stroll through the forest and leads you to a the old lead mines chimney

-The Mountain Acces Route (0.5kms, 30mins, moderate) is a linear trail that takes you to the top of Carrickgollogan hill with breathtaking views of the Dublin, Wicklow countryside and the Irish sea.

-Permanent Orienteering: Barnaslingan has 3 permanent orienteering courses, Short (1.7 km), Medium (2.4 km) and Advanced (2.4 km)


For more information visit carrickgollogan-walk

Wellness

Shankill GAA

Shankill GAA Club is Shankill’s local GAA Club and proudly promotes Gaelic Games in the heart of our community. The club caters for all ages and all skill levels, competing at Football, Hurling and Camogie. New members are always welcome at Shankill GAA Club!
For more information visit https://www.shankillgaa.ie/

Shankill Football Club

Shankill FC is a community based soccer club catering for boys and girls from age 4.
For more information visit https://www.shankillfc.ie/

Shankill Tennis Club

Fancy working on that forehand? Shankill Tennis club is a pay and play tennis club which allows members of the public to book courts and enjoy some tennis. With a mixture of indoor and outdoor courts,you can enjoy a game of tennis all year round.

Find out more at: https://shankilltennisclub.com/
